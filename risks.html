Risks

• System errors

• Machine malfunction

Potentially massive job loss could occur, with many being forced to move into different sectors and fields of work, potentially requiring years of training for many people. In addition to this, it could cause a glut in the job market, where some may not even be able to find new work due to an oversupply of newly available workers and not enough jobs to make up for the loss of those in agriculture.

One issue that people fear is the process of automation itself, and more succinctly, the displacement of people and communities totally geared towards agriculture being unwillingly compelled to learn new skills to retain their place in the agricultural workforce, or transfer to different fields of work. When making an autonomous system or machine, it is ultimately a balancing act where investing resources into developing one element of the automation means sacrificing resources from the other elements. Additionally, the transitional period from how agriculture is performed now to the implementation of automation would prove to be turbulent, as many changes would occur in rapid succession, effecting the people employed in the industry.
